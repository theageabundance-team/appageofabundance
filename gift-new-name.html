The New Name

Identity Protocol & The 6-Month Scripting

Twenty-one days. In Scripture, this number marks the end of a spiritual battle and the arrival of the answer. You are not the same person who began. Today, you receive your new name.

✦ ✦ ✦

In the Bible, whenever God transformed someone, one of the first things He did was change their name. Abram became Abraham — father of a multitude — before a single child had been born. Jacob, the deceiver, wrestled with God through the night and rose at dawn with a limp and a new name: Israel, the one who prevails with God. Saul, the persecutor, was blinded on a road and rose as Paul, the apostle to the nations.

A name is not just a label. In the Kingdom, a name is an identity, a calling, a declaration of what someone is — not what their past says, not what their circumstances suggest, but what God sees when He looks at them. For 21 days, He has been seeing something new in you. Today, you see it too.

Before you choose your name, you are going to do something important. You are going to read what you have already written.

Your Ritual
Three Acts of Transformation
Act One
Read Your Entire Golden Scroll
Before you do anything else today, go and find your Golden Scroll — the paper you have been carrying since Day 3. Sit somewhere quiet. Read everything you have written on it from the very beginning: the three victories you declared on Day 3, the flow you chose to activate on Day 7, the threats you surrendered on Day 15. Read it all, slowly.
What You Will Notice
As you read, notice what has shifted. Some of what you wrote may already be moving. Some of it may still feel distant — but notice that the person who wrote those words with trembling faith on Day 3 is not the same person reading them today. That distance is your transformation. Let yourself feel it before you move on.
Act Two
Choose Your New Name
Now you are going to choose a single word — your new name. Not a title, not a description. One word that captures who you have become in these 21 days. A word that, when you say it, you feel something true rising in your chest. It might be a virtue, a calling, a quality, or a declaration. There is no wrong answer — only the word that God has already been whispering.
Some words to consider — or let them inspire your own
Overcomer Heir Abundant Restored Faithful Chosen Protected Fruitful Renewed Established Redeemed Unshakeable
Your Instruction
Open your Golden Scroll. At the very top of the page — above everything else you have written — write your new name in the largest letters on the page. Underneath it, write: "This is who I am before God. This is the name He calls me."
Stand before the mirror once more.
Look into your eyes. Speak your new name aloud.

"This is my name before the Kingdom.
I am no longer who I was.
I receive this identity now."
Say it three times. The third time, let there be silence.
Act Three
Write the Next 6 Months
Now that you know who you are, it is time to write where you are going. This is the 6-Month Scripting — a powerful act of faith in which you write the story of the next six months of your life as if it has already happened. Not as wishes. Not as goals. As testimony. You are writing from the future, looking back at what God did.
Your Instruction
Take a new piece of paper — or the back of your Golden Scroll. Write today's date, then write: "Six months from today, this is what God has done:" Then write freely, guided by the prompts below. Do not filter. Do not be afraid of the size of what you write. God is not intimidated by big dreams.
Scripting Prompts — Write in past tense, as testimony
Finances & Provision
"Lord, thank You for what You did with my finances. Six months ago I could not see how — and then..."
Health & Strength
"My body has been renewed. I feel in my body what I declared in faith on Day 3..."
Relationships & Family
"The atmosphere in my home has changed. The people around me have noticed something different about me..."
Calling & Purpose
"The door that seemed closed has opened. I am now walking in what I was made for..."
Faith & Connection
"My relationship with God is not the same. I hear Him more clearly. I trust Him more deeply..."
