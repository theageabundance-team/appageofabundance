The Solomon Wealth Map

The 7 Sacred Flows of Abundance from Proverbs

Seven days ago, you began. Today, you receive what Solomon knew — the ancient map of how wealth truly moves in the Kingdom, hidden in plain sight inside the book of Proverbs.

✦ ✦ ✦

Solomon was the wealthiest man who ever lived. But his riches were not an accident, and they were not merely a divine favor granted without understanding. Solomon asked for wisdom — and wealth followed as a consequence. The book of Proverbs is the record of that wisdom, written so that those who came after him would not have to walk in ignorance.

What you are about to receive are the 7 flows Solomon identified — the channels through which abundance enters and exits a life. Most people are unknowingly blocking several of these flows at once. Read each one carefully. One of them will speak directly to your situation.

The Ancient Map
The 7 Flows of Abundance
I
The Flow of the Diligent Hand
Proverbs 10:4 — "Lazy hands make for poverty, but diligent hands bring wealth."
Solomon did not promise abundance to those who simply believed or prayed. He observed that wealth follows diligence the way water follows the slope of the land — consistently, faithfully, without exception. This is not about working harder. It is about working with intention, as someone who knows that their labor is a form of worship and an act of stewardship over what God has entrusted to them.
The Key
Show up fully for what is already in your hands. Heaven multiplies what is faithfully tended, not what is merely hoped for.
II
The Flow of the Generous Heart
Proverbs 11:24 — "One person gives freely, yet gains even more; another withholds unduly, but comes to poverty."
This is the flow that defies all human logic. Solomon observed something that cannot be explained by economics alone: those who give generously create an opening in their lives through which more can enter. Generosity is not a loss — it is a declaration that you trust the Source more than you trust the supply. It breaks the spirit of scarcity and aligns the soul with the nature of God, who is Himself an inexhaustible giver.
The Key
Give from a place of faith, not from what is left over. The widow's offering was small in quantity and vast in its power — because it was given first.
III
The Flow of Wise Counsel
Proverbs 15:22 — "Plans fail for lack of counsel, but with many advisers they succeed."
Solomon built an empire not by knowing everything himself, but by surrounding himself with those who knew what he did not. Isolation is one of the most common and overlooked causes of financial stagnation. The flow of abundance is often waiting just on the other side of a conversation you have been too proud, too fearful, or too busy to have. Wisdom is not weakness. Seeking counsel is the posture of kings.
The Key
Identify one area of your finances or calling where you have been moving alone. That is exactly where you need a voice of wisdom beside you.
IV
The Flow of the Guarded Mouth
Proverbs 21:23 — "Those who guard their mouths and their tongues keep themselves from calamity."
Few people understand how much their words are shaping their financial reality. The tongue is not neutral — it is either building or dismantling. Every declaration of "I will always be broke," every agreement with lack, every complaint spoken as a permanent truth is a brick placed in a wall between you and your abundance. Solomon understood this deeply. The mouth is a gate — and you decide what passes through it.
The Key
For the next 7 days, notice every word you speak about money, provision, and lack. What you consistently declare is what you consistently invite.
V
The Flow of Sacred Stewardship
Proverbs 27:23 — "Be sure you know the condition of your flocks, give careful attention to your herds."
Solomon observed that abundance does not stay where it is not watched over. Stewardship is the art of knowing what you have, honoring it, and tending it with the same seriousness you would bring to a sacred trust — because that is exactly what it is. God does not give more to those who do not know what they already have. To be trusted with greater things, you must first demonstrate faithfulness over the present ones.
The Key
Know the condition of your resources today — not anxiously, but as a faithful steward. Clarity precedes multiplication.
VI
The Flow of Patient Planting
Proverbs 13:11 — "Wealth gained hastily will dwindle, but whoever gathers little by little will increase it."
The kingdom of abundance operates on the principle of the seed, not the lottery. What grows slowly grows deep. Solomon warned against the seduction of quick riches — not because abundance should be slow, but because anything built without roots cannot withstand the storms that come to test what has been built. The most fruitful trees are also the ones that took the longest to grow. Plant well. Water consistently. Trust the process that cannot be rushed.
The Key
Identify the seed you are planting right now. Tend it with patience and consistency. A tree does not apologize for the time it takes to bear fruit.
VII
The Flow of Divine Honor
Proverbs 3:9-10 — "Honor the Lord with your wealth, with the firstfruits of all your crops; then your barns will be filled to overflowing."
This is the foundation beneath all other flows. Solomon placed it not last because it is least important, but because it is the one that holds all others together. When God is honored first — not from what remains, but from the first and the best — a supernatural principle is activated. The barns overflow not as a transaction, but as the natural consequence of a life that has placed the Source above the supply. This is not religion. This is the architecture of Kingdom abundance.
The Key
What does it look like for you to honor God first in your finances this week? Not from what is left — from what arrives first.
